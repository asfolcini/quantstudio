# 8. NEWS ANALYSIS

## Why Use the News Analysis Module?
Markets often react to macroeconomic news, earnings reports, or geopolitical events. However:
- **Too much noise**: Most news doesn’t move markets—filtering is critical.
- **Human bias**: Emotional interpretation clouds judgment.

The **News Analysis** module **automates this process**: it scans global RSS feeds (Google News, Reuters) and uses LLM AI to identify news with **real financial impact**.

---

## How It Works *(Simple Explanation)*

### 1. **How Are News Items Classified?**
Every news item is analyzed and categorized based on two criteria:

| Criteria          | Key Question                             | Example                                                                                 |
|-------------------|------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Informational Value** | Does the news contain concrete data?      | ✅ "Apple beats earnings estimates" <br> ❌ "Experts discuss Apple stock" |
| **Market Impact**       | How might markets react?                | ✅ "ECB raises rates" → Impact on banks/EUR <br> ❌ "ECB press conference today" |

---

### 2. **Impact Score: What Does It Mean?**
The LLM assigns an **Impact Score** (1–10) to each news item, based on:
- **Event Severity**:
  - *8–10*: Geopolitical crises, earnings collapses, sovereign defaults (e.g., "credit default swap meltdown").
  - *4–7*: Macroeconomic data (e.g., US inflation), earnings surprises.
  - *1–3*: Contextual news (e.g., political debates, speculative analysis).

- **Asset Liquidity**:
  News about **MicroStrategy** (low liquidity) scores lower than news about **Apple** (high liquidity), even for similar events.

> **💡 Real-World Examples**:
> - **"Nvidia launches revolutionary AI chip"** → **Impact Score 9** (tech sector, high volume, tickers: NVDA, AMD).
> - **"Politician comments on US-China tensions"** → **Impact Score 3** (opinion, no direct impact).

---

### 3. **High-Impact Analysis: What Will You See?**
In the TUI (**Option 8 → 1**), the module displays **only news with Impact Score ≥6**, sorted by priority. For each news item:

```
📰 [NEWS HEADLINE]
   • Impact Score: 8/10 🔴  *(red = critical impact)*
   • Sectors Affected: Technology, Semiconductors
   • Tickers: NVDA (+3%), AMD (+1.5%), SMH (+2%)
   • Reason: "New GPU mandatory for data center upgrades"
```

> **Why "≥6"?**
> - **6–7**: Events likely to shift sector sentiment (e.g., CPI data in line with expectations).
> - **8+**: Potential catalysts for rapid price movements (e.g., unexpected Fed rate cuts).

---

### 4. **How to Use This Information**
| Scenario          | Recommendation                                                                                     |
|-------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Day Trading**   | Use **≥8 Impact Scores** to identify intraday catalysts (e.g., earnings surprises).               |
| **Swing Trading** | Monitor **≥6 Impact Scores** to adjust sector exposure based on sentiment shifts.                 |
| **Risk Management** | For **≥9 Impact Scores**, consider wider stop-losses or hedging strategies.                       |
| **Ignore Noise**      | News with **score <6** are typically market rumors—filter these out.                             |

---

### 5. Limitations and Warnings

#### ❌ What the Module **Does NOT Do**:
- **Not a trading system**: Provides **situational awareness**, not trade signals.
- **Cannot predict the future**: Impact Scores are based on known patterns (e.g., "Fed cuts impact banks").
- **LLM bias**: Ambiguous news may receive over/underestimated scores.

#### ✅ How to Maximize Accuracy:
1. **Configure RSS feeds** (`config/config.json`) with trusted sources (e.g., Reuters, Bloomberg).
2. **Cross-reference with other tools**: Combine with technical analysis (e.g., [Statistical Edge Engine](2.%20Statistical%20Edge%20Engine.md)).
3. **Trust but verify**: LLM may over-index on sensational headlines—use your judgment (e.g., "CEO tweets" vs. "Company lays off 20%").

---

### 6. FAQ

#### **How Do I Configure RSS Feeds?**
In `config/config.json`, under the `news` section, add region-specific RSS feeds. Example:
```json
"news": {
  "Europe": ["https://news.google.com/rss?hl=en&gl=GB&ceid=GB:en"],
  "Crypto": ["https://feed.crypto-news.example/rss"]
}
```

#### **Is the Impact Score Perfect?**
No. It’s a **probabilistic model** with ~5–10% error rate. Examples:
- **Unexpected events** (earthquakes, coups) may be underestimated.
- **Ambiguous news** (political statements) may receive inconsistent scores.

#### **Can I Change the Threshold (e.g., ≥7 instead of ≥6)?**
Currently, the threshold is fixed for consistency. Future updates may allow customization.

## Key Takeaways
The News Analysis module **cuts through the noise** and surfaces **only what matters**, using LLM AI to assign **objective Impact Scores**. It doesn’t replace your judgment but **amplifies it**, enabling you to:
✔️ React quickly to high-impact events.
✔️ Avoid information overload.
✔️ Integrate qualitative insights into quantitative strategies.
